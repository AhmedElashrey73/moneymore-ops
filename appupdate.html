What we design vs. Play
  • Ours: soft nudge (trigger), the “ready to install” bar, and the hard-gate backstop.
  • Play-owned (dashed placeholder frames, not our UI): confirmation dialog, background download, install + restart.
  • Home never shows download progress. The nudge just launches Play’s flexible flow and doesn’t change state.
Ready-to-install bar (required)
  • Slim bar above the tab bar — not a modal. Flexible updates don’t self-install; this is what triggers it.
  • If dismissed, it re-appears on next foreground while the update is pending (use the dashed “simulate app resume” pill).
Hard gate = backstop
  • CTA launches Play’s immediate flow. If the user backs out, they land back on the same, unchanged blocking screen — no scolding, no error.
  • Play unavailable → gate persists with an Open Play Store fallback CTA.
  • No progress / downloading / restart states — Play owns all of it.
Decisions & tweaks
  • Top floating banner rejected. Prototype the two survivors: A inline (default) vs B bottom-floating — the winner ships identically on both platforms.
  • Hard-gate art: glowing emblem vs minimal icon — hardGateArt tweak.
  • reducedMotion shows the static fallback for every entrance.
