Impact levels — shared language for every conversation

Four levels. One shared vocabulary.

Every monitored experience is tagged with one of these four levels. Use this language in standups, sprint reviews, and Slack. When engineering posts an alert update, they use these exact words.

App unusable
Core action completely broken
Happening to all or most users. No workaround exists. Engineering drops everything. Fix or rollback same day.
Broken flow
A specific journey is broken end-to-end
A subset of users cannot complete a flow. Acknowledge same day. Fix within 48 hours or sprint Fix Track.
Degraded UX
Works but users notice something wrong
Slow, inconsistent, or partially incorrect. Ticket in Fix Track if not immediately fixable.
Worth watching
Trending wrong, not breaking yet
No immediate action. Surface in the weekly signal update and review at sprint close.
Required communication format

How engineering tells us when something breaks

When an alert fires, engineering posts the following in the Slack channel. This is not optional. The Fix size field is the most important one — it tells product whether this goes into the current sprint, the Fix Track backlog, or needs escalation.

Post this in the channel every time an alert fires — no exceptions
