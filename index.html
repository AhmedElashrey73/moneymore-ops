Discovering the future
Gates 1 and 2. Continuous work — not a scheduled event.
Product
Gate 1 — Problem definition
Is there a real user problem with at least two independent sources of evidence? If yes, it enters the pipeline. If not, it does not proceed.
Product
Gate 2 — Evidence check + RICE
Evidence strength rated Strong, Moderate, or Weak. Weak means stop. RICE score calculated — Effort estimate comes from Eng Lead. Feature enters Evidence Backlog only if Moderate or Strong.
Mktg
Signal review (daily)
Growth Marketing and Product review all user signals from the sprint together — NPS, support tickets, interviews, app store reviews. Patterns identified. Research Archive updated. Evidence Backlog scores upgraded where new data changes the picture.
Product
Monthly experience audit
Five-layer audit of all primary flows — Clarity, Confidence, Friction, Delight, Polish. Score 1 findings go directly into Fix Track Backlog as Fix Briefs.

All recurring touchpoints
Every sprint · Day 1 · 90 min
Sprint planning
ProductEngineeringDesign
Full team. Engineering self-assigns Stories. Engineers create their own Tasks under each Story — product does not assign technical work. Sprint board populated by end of session.
Output: Sprint board live. Everyone knows what they are building and why.
Every sprint · Days 3 + 8 · 20 min
Wednesday touchpoint
ProductEngineering
Blockers raised — not stored in heads. Product makes decisions on the spot or commits to 24 hours. No status updates. No slides. If there are no blockers, the meeting ends in five minutes.
Output: Blockers cleared. Sprint continues without interruption.
Every sprint · Days 1–5 · 30–45 min per feature
Gate 3 feasibility session
ProductEng LeadDesign Lead
Product presents the proposed solution as a user flow in words — not a Figma, not a brief. Eng Lead gives a feasibility read and rough size. Design Lead confirms scope. This is not a discussion of whether to build — that was Gate 2. Scheduled by product at sprint start, must happen by Day 5.
Output: Feasibility confirmed. Design Brief can be written. Feature moves to Gate 4.
Every sprint · Day 9 · 60 min
Pre-sprint planning
ProductEng Lead
Product presents all next sprint Stories with fields complete — acceptance criteria, Figma link, definition of done. Eng Lead reviews, confirms sizing, flags any blockers. Capacity confirmed at 35% Fix / 65% Build. MoSCoW verified — Must Have cannot exceed 60% of capacity.
Output: Accepted sprint backlog. Engineering starts Day 1 with everything they need.
Every sprint · Day 10 · 60 min
Sprint review + retrospective
ProductEngineeringDesignGrowth Mktg
Review: what shipped versus what was committed. Delivery rate logged — no blame, just data. Retro: what worked, what did not. One process change agreed and committed. Not a list — one specific change that takes effect next sprint.
Output: Delivery data logged. One improvement committed.
Every sprint · Day 5 · 45 min
Signal review
ProductGrowth Mktg
Growth Marketing and Product sit together and review all user signals from the sprint — NPS responses, support tickets, interview clips, app store reviews. Not just reading them — identifying patterns and asking what they tell us about the backlog. Research Archive updated. Evidence Backlog scores upgraded where new signals change the picture.
Output: Research Archive entries written. Backlog evidence updated.
Monthly · Start of month
Experience audit
Product
Product scores all primary flows against the five-layer audit: Clarity, Confidence, Friction, Delight, and Polish. Any flow scoring 1 generates a Fix Brief that goes directly into the Fix Track Backlog. This is the primary source of Fix Track items.
Output: Fix Track items logged. Audit scores recorded.
Monthly · Mid-month
Metrics review
ProductGrowth Mktg
North star dashboard reviewed. Growth model actuals replace assumptions. Experiment results assessed. Backlog priorities confirmed or adjusted against real data — not gut feel.
Output: Growth model updated. Backlog reprioritised if data warrants it.
