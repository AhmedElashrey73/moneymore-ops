Budgets — iOS
Category-level spending limits on iOS, where bank SMS auto-categorisation isn't possible. Users get there through manual entries or the iOS Shortcut (enabled elsewhere in the app — Home and Transactions). Suggested Budgets unlock after 30 days of activity.
The problem
iOS doesn't allow background SMS reads, so the Android pattern of automatic categorisation breaks. The user gets data into MoneyMore by logging entries by hand or by enabling the iOS Shortcut (prompted from Home and Transactions, not here). The Budgets tab's job is to turn that data into useful limits — first manually, then through 30-day suggestions that come from real spending patterns.

Filter chips (All / On Limit / Near Limit / Over Limit) sit between Suggested Budgets and the budget list, scrollable horizontally. Tapping a chip filters only the budget list below — never the overview cards or the suggestions slot.

Each suggestion is its own row with a dashed gold border (matches the countdown's visual treatment, signals "not yet a real budget"). Two action buttons per row: green check (accept → opens modal) and outline × (dismiss). Both are 34×34 visually, 44×44 tap target.
Suggestions sit above filter chips and the budget list. Order: Spending Overview → Suggested Budgets → filter chips → budget list. Suggestions go away when accepted (after modal save) or dismissed.
Flow 4 — Accept a suggestion
Tapping the green check on a suggestion opens a bottom-sheet modal with the suggested amount pre-filled. The user can edit the amount, then save. The modal is the create-budget moment — same primitives as the standard create-budget flow (DB-010), but with the category locked and the amount pre-filled.

Accessibility — what changes what you draw
  • Touch targets — 44×44px minimum on every interactive element. Critical here for the ✓ and × buttons on suggestion rows (visually 34×34, must hit 44×44 tap area), the filter chips, the bell icon, and the modal's drag handle.
  • Contrast — 4.5:1 minimum for body text, 7:1 for financial figures (budget amounts, spend totals, EGP values). Verify the 7:1 against the navy filter-chip-active state, the green/gold/red status pills, and the muted "of 2,000 EGP" text on budget rows.
  • Never colour-only for state. Status pills use both colour and label text ("On Limit", "Near Limit", "Over Limit"); progress bars need that label nearby. Spending Overview cards combine count, label, and bar — never strip to colour-only.
  • Focus order — top-to-bottom, even on RTL. Title → bell → Spending Overview cards (left-to-right or RTL flipped) → Suggested Budgets → filter chips → budget list. Within each suggestion row: name → accept → dismiss.
  • Dynamic count in countdown headline (Flow 2) needs careful RTL handling — the {X} variable sits inside the sentence on both EN and AR, and Arabic numerals are LTR within an RTL paragraph. Confirm with native speaker review that the direction marks are right.
