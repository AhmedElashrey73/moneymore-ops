Play Store Screenshots — 8 frames
Eight Play Store screenshots in two complete sets — English (LTR) and Arabic (RTL). Final visual spec for designer to match in production assets. Each frame is a marketing composition: brand background + headline + subhead + phone mockup, sized to Play Store aspect ratio.
The problem
Play Store users decide in seven seconds. The first three frames are visible without scrolling and must answer "what is this, why is it different, why now" in three glances. Most expense-tracker downloads are lost to the subscription objection and the trust objection — these screenshots have to neutralise both before the user looks elsewhere.

Accessibility — what changes what you draw
  • Contrast — 4.5:1 minimum for headline text on background. Check every frame, especially Frame 3 (cream/gold background) and Frame 6 (green background) where readability degrades fastest. Run each colour pair through a contrast tool before finalising.
  • 7:1 for in-app financial figures. EGP amounts, percentages, and balance numbers inside the phone mockup must clear the higher bar — these are the proof points.
  • Never colour-only for state. The "close to limit" budget bar in Frame 5 needs the warning amber colour and the words "close to limit" — the gold bar alone reads as decorative.
  • Headline at thumbnail size. Play Store renders these at ~150px wide in search results. Headline must remain legible at that size — keep to ≤6 words, weight 700+, no thin display fonts.
  • Arabic font pairing. Cairo or IBM Plex Sans Arabic at weights 700/800. The font in the prototype is Cairo as a placeholder — confirm the production pick before locking the asset.
Notes for production assets
  • Final asset dimensions. Play Store phone screenshots are 1080×1920px (9:16). The prototype frame is shown at smaller scale here; designer renders at full resolution.
  • Two complete sets, not one with translated overlay. The Arabic phone mockup content (merchant names, category labels, numerals) differs from the English version. Do not reuse the English image and swap the headline.
  • Consistent phone position across all 8 frames. Same vertical offset, same tilt, same shadow. The eye should recognise all 8 as a set immediately.
  • Numerals. In-app content uses Eastern Arabic numerals (٠١٢٣٤٥٦٧٨٩) in the Arabic set, Western in the English set. EGP currency abbreviated as "EGP" in English, "ج.م." in Arabic.
  • Frame 7 palette is intentionally cooler. The trust frame uses a desaturated navy-grey, distinct from the brand navy in Frames 5 and 8. Keep that distinction.
  • Order matters. If frames must be cut, drop in this sequence: 8 → 6 → 4. Frames 1, 2, 3, 5, 7 are load-bearing for ASO conversion logic.
